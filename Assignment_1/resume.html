Project

✓ B.E. Project : Study of sustainable Mortar Containing Brick powder Waste

Language : English

The construction industry is one of the largest consumer industry of natural resources and contribute to environmental degradation. In past several years, there has been a awareness is spread in communities of need of sustainable construction practices to mitigate the environmental impacts. This study focuses on development of eco-friendly and sustainable construction material that can reduce resource consumption and waste generation.

Mortar is a fundamental building construction material used for binding of bricks, stones, and other units together. Mortar is one of the most important material used in the building construction and rehabilitation. It plays a crucial role in providing structural durability and weather resistance to masonry structure. It is a mixture of cement and sand and portable water. It is commonly used for general work like bricklaying, filler material in joints and plastering work.

In India the rate of building demolition waste is 150 million tonnes per year and it accounts 35% to 40% of the global construction and demolition waste annually. In India only 1% of the demolition waste is recycled according to the center for science and environment estimates. This construction waste includes steel, glass, wood and brick waste etc.

This project aims to address the challenges by experimenting potential of recycled brick powder as a sustainable alternative in mortar. Brick manufacturing plant is a source of unused broken bricks and demolition of building waste is being sent to the landfills each year. Recycling this waste into powder and converting it into mortar offers a promising solution to reduce environmental impact while enhancing the performance of construction material.
